4. Bell's Inequality: A Topological Necessity

4.1 Bell's Theorem in One Paragraph

Bell's theorem (1964) proves that no local hidden variable theory — where each particle carries a predetermined outcome for every measurement — can reproduce all quantum predictions. The CHSH inequality \(|S| \leq 2\) holds for all local hidden variable models. Quantum mechanics predicts \(|S| \leq 2\sqrt{2}\) (Tsirelson bound), with the maximum achieved for spin-½ singlet states. Experiments confirm the quantum prediction. The question is why K6/QS3 agrees — and the answer is topological.

4.2 The Hopf Bundle Obstruction

In K6/QS3, spin-½ particles live on QS3₂ = SU(2) = S³. A measurement direction is a point on the Bloch sphere S². A local hidden variable model needs a global section of the Hopf bundle — a continuous map \(s: S^2\to S^3\) with \(p\circ s = \mathrm{id}\). Such a section would split the bundle, implying \(c_1 = 0\). But:

The Hopf bundle has \(c_1 = 1 \neq 0\): no global section exists. Bell's inequality must be violated in K6/QS3. This is a theorem, not a calculation.

The Hopf bundle sequence: \(S^1\hookrightarrow S^3 \xrightarrow{p} S^2\).

4.3 CHSH at the Tsirelson Bound

The singlet correlation function from QS3 SU(2) structure is \(E(\mathbf{a},\mathbf{b}) = -\cos\theta_{ab}\). At optimal angles \((a=0°, a'=90°, b=45°, b'=135°)\):

\[ S = E(a,b)-E(a,b')+E(a',b)+E(a',b') = -2\sqrt{2} \qquad |S| = 2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.828 \]

The classical bound of 2 is exceeded. The Tsirelson bound is saturated. Bell violation holds for all measurement angles \(b\) between 10° and 85° (verified numerically).
