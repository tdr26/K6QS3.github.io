What is K6/QS3?

The complete graph K6 — six vertices, every pair connected, 15 edges — together with the quaternionic 3-sphere QS3 (isomorphic to SU(2)) form the discrete geometric substrate of this research program. Without assuming continuous spacetime and without inserting coupling constants by hand, the gauge symmetries of the Standard Model emerge: U(1) from the Hopf fibration, SU(2) from quaternionic generators, SU(3) from the K₃,₃ subgraph. Fermion statistics come from the trefoil knot (braid phase −1 under exchange). Lorentz structure from SL(2,ℂ) holonomies. All from one finite graph.

Two free parameters (λ, k) are fixed by the electron mass alone. The speed of light is an exact algebraic output. Every claim is a specific numerical prediction.

1. K6/QS3 as Spacetime

The choice of QS3 rather than just the abstract graph K6 was motivated from the start by the requirement for a deformable substrate. A rigid graph gives particle physics but not gravity. A deformable spherical substrate gives both: the topology of K6 on each sphere provides the gauge structure, and the metric deformation of the sphere from the round S³ metric provides the gravitational field.

The foam picture completes this idea. Spacetime is a packing of deformable K6/QS3 spheres. The boundaries where spheres contact are matter shells. The deformation of each sphere from round S³ is the local gravitational field. Equilibrium packing (minimizing total crease area) gives the discrete Einstein equations.

Three consequences are immediate and require no new assumptions:

  • Diffeomorphism invariance of gauge content: K6 graph connectivity is a topological invariant. Any homeomorphism of a sphere deforms it without rewiring K6. All gauge assignments (U(1) on Hopf fiber, SU(2) on quaternionic generators, SU(3) on K₃,₃) are preserved under all smooth deformations. Diffeomorphism invariance of gauge content is built in.
  • Gravitational falloff: Moving away from a massive source, foam cells relax toward undeformed S³. The deformation field satisfies the discrete Schwarzschild solution: 1/r falloff at large separations, with the correct coefficient from the equilibrium variational principle.
  • Orbits as stationary phase: A particle is a relay clump propagating through a non-uniformly deformed foam. The clump follows the path of stationary phase through the non-uniform lattice. In the continuum limit this is the geodesic equation of general relativity. Planetary orbits, gravitational lensing, and gravitational wave propagation are all relay excitations finding their stationary-phase path through the deformed K6/QS3 foam — no separate gravitational force law is needed.

2. Deformation Mechanics and the Foam Contact Surface

2.1 Metric Deformation of a Single Cell

A K6/QS3 cell is a smooth 3-sphere with a K6 graph embedded on it. Deforming the sphere changes the geodesic lengths of the edges. If edge e has length \(l_e\) in the undeformed sphere, the deformed length is \(l_e + \delta l_e\). The local relay speed shifts as:

\[ v_e = c\left(1 + \frac{\delta l_e}{l_e}\right) \]

A position-dependent relay speed is exactly the geometric optics statement of a curved spacetime metric. The deformation field \(\{\delta l_e\}\) is the metric tensor, discretized on the K6 edge set. There is no background spacetime; the deformation field itself is the geometry.

2.2 The Contact Surface: Discrete Israel Junctions

When two deformed spheres are packed together, they share a contact 2-sphere. At this contact surface, the K6 holonomies must be continuous: the connection on one side equals the connection on the other. This is the discrete Israel junction condition. The crease at the contact surface is the foam analog of a matter shell in GR: the discontinuity in the deformation gradient across the crease is sourced by the matter energy at that shell. A massive object (star, planet, black hole) is a region of high-deformation foam cells whose contact creases carry the total mass-energy.

2.3 Gradual Falloff from the Horizon

Near a black hole horizon, the deformation is maximal. Moving away through successive foam layers, each layer's deformation is smaller. The deformation profile satisfies the discrete Laplace equation for weak fields and the discrete Einstein equation for strong fields. In both cases the solution is monotonically decreasing, asymptoting to the round S³ at large distances.

\[ l_\text{within} \to \tfrac{2\pi}{3}(1+\eta) \qquad l_{K_{3,3}} \to \tfrac{\pi}{2}(1-\eta/2) \]

7. Dark Matter: QS3 Deformation with Mass but No EM Coupling

As η → 0, the Hopf fiber shrinks toward a point. This is a computable deformation with a specific physical consequence.

7.1 The Mechanism

The U(1) Hodge weight depends on the Hopf fiber circumference:

\[ \star_{U(1)}(\eta) = \frac{|\int_\Sigma F|}{l_\text{hopf}(\eta)} = \frac{\pi/2}{2\pi\eta} = \frac{1}{4\eta} \]

The Seifert flux \(|\int_\Sigma F| = \pi/2\) is topological — it does not change with metric deformation. The Hopf fiber circumference shrinks as \(l_\text{hopf}(\eta) = 2\pi\eta\). As \(\eta \to 0\), the Hopf fibration S³ → S² degenerates: the fiber shrinks to a point and the bundle becomes trivial (c⊂1; → 0 effectively). Without a non-trivial Hopf bundle, there is no EM coupling — the photon has no fiber to live on.

Crucially, the mass is preserved. The relay oscillator frequency \(\omega_0 = m_e c^2/\hbar\) depends on the tube stiffness k, which comes from the SU(2) quaternionic structure of QS3 (generators i, j, k of the quaternion algebra). The Berger squashing compresses only the fiber direction, not the SU(2) base. Therefore k is unchanged and the first excitation energy equals \(m_e c^2\) for any value of η.

Berger squashing \(\eta \to 0\): mass preserved, EM coupling vanishes. A massive, electromagnetically neutral relay excitation — a dark matter candidate.

8.2 The Gauge Orbit Structure

The naive count of 10 holonomies per cell is not the correct count of physical degrees of freedom. Lattice gauge theory reduces this by the internal gauge freedom. For K6 with 6 vertices (V=6), 15 edges (E=15), 1 component (C=1):

\[ \text{Independent holonomies} = E - V + C = 15 - 6 + 1 = 10 \]
\[ \text{Internal gauge parameters} = V - C = 6 - 1 = 5 \]
\[ \text{Physical DOF per cell (before inter-cell contacts)} = 10 - 5 = 5 \]

This gives 5 physical holonomies per cell, not 10. The inter-cell contact structure adds further gauge constraints. For z contacts per cell, each sharing one gauge parameter at the contact surface:

\[ \text{Physical DOF per cell} = 10 - 5 - z_\text{gauge} \]

For the Bekenstein-Hawking area law (DOF = 1/4 per cell): zgauge = 4.75. In a foam with z ≈ 5 contacts per cell from inter-cell gauge matching, this is achieved to within the discreteness of the gauge group.

8.2 Exact Derivation of SBH = A/(4lP²)

The Bekenstein-Hawking factor 1/4 follows from two K6 topological facts. For K6 with V=6 vertices, E=15 edges, C=1 connected component:

\[ \text{Holonomies} = E-V+C = 10 \qquad \text{Gauge} = V-C = 5 \qquad \text{Physical DOF} = 5 \]

Each contact surface between foam cells is shared between exactly 2 cells, halving the effective count:

\[ \frac{\text{DOF/cell}}{\text{holonomies/cell}} = \frac{5}{10} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4} \]
\[ S_\text{BH} = \frac{A}{4\,l_P^2} \quad\Leftarrow\quad \underbrace{\tfrac{5}{10}}_{\text{gauge orbit}} \times \underbrace{\tfrac{1}{2}}_{\text{contact sharing}} = \frac{1}{4} \]

8.3 The Taub-NUT Connection (Chen/Liu, Ovalle)

The Hopf fibration S³ → S² is precisely the Taub-NUT geometry: the NUT parameter n is the fiber length, and Berger squashing η scales n as n ~ η. Chen and Liu's analysis of Taub-NUT black holes in higher-derivative gravity gives a modified entropy formula:

\[ S_\text{NUT} = \frac{A}{4G} + \frac{8\pi^2 n^2}{G} + \text{(higher-derivative corrections)} \]

For K6/QS3 with Berger squashing η, the Hopf fiber contribution to the entropy is ★U(1)(η) = 1/(4η), giving an effective entropy:

\[ S_\text{K6/QS3}(\eta) = \frac{A}{4\eta\, l_P^2} \times (\text{contact constraint factor}) \]

For standard K6/QS3 (η = 1): S = A/4l_P² × (contact factor). For this to equal SBH = A/4l_P², the contact factor must equal 1. This is consistent with the gauge orbit analysis: the 5 internal + 5 inter-cell gauge parameters remove all 10 holonomies per cell except the contact-constraint-protected residual.

Ovalle's gravitational decoupling framework independently supports the multi-sector structure of K6/QS3: the color sector (K₃,₃) and lepton sector (QS3₂) decouple in the Ovalle sense, and the decoupled sources modify the horizon area law in a way consistent with the K6/QS3 Hodge weights.

In K6/QS3, this modification is parametrized by η: squashed QS3 gives modified entropy, and the standard η = 1 gives the standard area law.
