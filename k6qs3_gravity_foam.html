1. K6/QS3 as Spacetime

The choice of QS3 rather than just the abstract graph K6 was motivated from the start by the requirement for a deformable substrate. A rigid graph gives particle physics but not gravity. A deformable spherical substrate gives both: the topology of K6 on each sphere provides the gauge structure, and the metric deformation of the sphere from the round S³ metric provides the gravitational field.

The foam picture completes this idea. Spacetime is a packing of deformable K6/QS3 spheres. The boundaries where spheres contact are matter shells. The deformation of each sphere from round S³ is the local gravitational field. Equilibrium packing (minimizing total crease area) gives the discrete Einstein equations. Three consequences are immediate and require no new assumptions:

  • Diffeomorphism invariance of gauge content: K6 graph connectivity is a topological invariant. Any homeomorphism of a sphere deforms it without rewiring K6. All gauge assignments — U(1) on Hopf fiber, SU(2) on quaternionic generators, SU(3) on K\(_{3,3}\) — are preserved under all smooth deformations.
  • Gravitational falloff: Moving away from a massive source, foam cells relax toward undeformed S³. The deformation field satisfies the discrete Schwarzschild solution: 1/r falloff at large separations, with the correct coefficient from the equilibrium variational principle.
  • Orbits as stationary phase: A particle is a relay clump propagating through a non-uniformly deformed foam. The clump follows the path of stationary phase — in the continuum limit this is the geodesic equation of general relativity. Planetary orbits, gravitational lensing, and gravitational wave propagation are all relay excitations finding their stationary-phase path through the deformed K6/QS3 foam. No separate gravitational force law is needed.

2. Deformation Mechanics and the Foam Contact Surface

2.1 Metric Deformation of a Single Cell

A K6/QS3 cell is a smooth 3-sphere with a K6 graph embedded on it. Deforming the sphere changes the geodesic lengths of the edges. If edge \(e\) has length \(l_e\) in the undeformed sphere, the deformed length is \(l_e + \delta l_e\). The local relay speed shifts as:

\[v_e = c\!\left(1 + \frac{\delta l_e}{l_e}\right)\]

A position-dependent relay speed is exactly the geometric optics statement of a curved spacetime metric. The deformation field \(\{\delta l_e\}\) is the metric tensor, discretized on the K6 edge set. There is no background spacetime; the deformation field itself is the geometry.

2.2 The Contact Surface: Discrete Israel Junctions

When two deformed spheres are packed together, they share a contact 2-sphere. At this contact surface, the K6 holonomies must be continuous: the connection on one side equals the connection on the other. This is the discrete Israel junction condition. The crease at the contact surface is the foam analog of a matter shell in GR: the discontinuity in the deformation gradient across the crease is sourced by the matter energy at that shell. A massive object is a region of high-deformation foam cells whose contact creases carry the total mass-energy.

2.3 Gradual Falloff from the Horizon

Near a black hole horizon, the deformation is maximal. Moving away through successive foam layers, each layer's deformation is smaller. The deformation profile satisfies the discrete Laplace equation for weak fields and the discrete Einstein equation for strong fields. In both cases the solution is monotonically decreasing, asymptoting to the round S³ at large distances.

3. Vacuum QS3 Squashing and the Alpha Residual

The K6/QS3 calculations in Papers 1–4 assume the undeformed round S³ metric. This is the idealized vacuum. In a foam packing with non-trivial global structure, the background metric on each cell is not exactly round. The deviation is the Berger squashing parameter \(\eta\): a one-parameter deformation of S³ that preserves SU(2) × U(1) but breaks SO(4). A squashed S³ has two edge-length classes:

\[l_\text{within} \to \tfrac{2\pi}{3}(1+\eta) \qquad l_{K_{3,3}} \to \tfrac{\pi}{2}(1-\eta/2)\]

This shifts the Hodge star weights and feeds into the alpha correction:

\[\Delta(1/\alpha)_\text{squash} \approx -\frac{\eta}{3\pi}\bigl[6\ln(1+\eta) + 9\ln(1-\eta/2)\bigr]\]

For the residual gap of 0.854 in \(1/\alpha\) remaining after three completed corrections (Paper 3), the required squashing is \(\eta \approx 0.84\). This is a large deformation and should be treated as a conjecture pending independent verification. The direction is correct and the mechanism is physically motivated. The Berger parameter \(\eta\) is directly measurable in Weyl semimetal experiments via the Hall impedance \(Z_H = R_K\cdot\eta\); see Paper 4, Section 8.

4. Interior-Exterior Entanglement: The Foam Mechanism

In the K6/QS3 foam, there is no topological disconnection at the horizon. Interior cells are connected to exterior cells through the contact topology: each cell is identified with its neighbors at contact surfaces, and this chain propagates continuously from the deep interior through the horizon to the exterior. K6 is 5-edge-connected: 5 simultaneous edge failures are required before any vertex loses connectivity to any other. Local thermal fluctuations cannot cause 5 simultaneous failures at the same vertex. The interior-exterior topological connection is therefore thermodynamically stable.

Information is stored in topological integers (holonomy winding numbers, Wilson loops) which are invariant under any smooth deformation of the interior. Information is preserved through the horizon because topology is preserved through the horizon; metric (photon paths) is not.

5. Soft Hair: K6/QS3 Holonomies at the Horizon

5.1 The Hawking-Perry-Strominger Proposal

Hawking, Perry, and Strominger (2016) proposed that black holes carry "soft hair" — imprints on the horizon of every infalling particle, encoded in soft graviton modes associated with the BMS supertranslation symmetry. The proposal lacked a microscopic mechanism: why does the horizon carry these degrees of freedom, and how are all particle properties (not just position) encoded? K6/QS3 provides the mechanism.

5.2 The Mechanism

Each infalling particle is a relay clump that deforms the contact topology of the foam cells it passes through. As it crosses the horizon, it shifts the K6 holonomies at the outermost foam layer contact surfaces. These shifts are topological integers — they cannot be erased by any smooth deformation of the interior. The soft hair is the complete set of K6 holonomy shifts at the horizon, organized by gauge sector.

6. Black Hole Evaporation: Topological Phase Transition

As a black hole evaporates by Hawking radiation, its temperature rises. At the critical mass:

\[M_c = \frac{M_\text{threshold}}{8\pi\,\star_\text{within}} = \frac{m_P^2/m_e}{12\arccos(1/\sqrt{10})} \approx 3.5\times10^{13}\;\text{kg}\]

the Hawking temperature equals the characteristic energy of the within-group edges. Below this mass, the within-group edges (carrying Fermi statistics) begin thermally fluctuating. The evaporation sequence is a topological phase transition: Standard Model structure at the horizon dissolves in the specific order dictated by the Hodge weights — Fermi statistics first, then electromagnetism, then color. All astrophysical black holes are far above this mass and have fully frozen K6 at their horizons.

8.2 Exact Derivation of \(S_\text{BH} = A/(4l_P^2)\)

For K6 with \(V=6\), \(E=15\), \(C=1\):

\[\text{Independent holonomies} = E - V + C = 10 \qquad \text{Internal gauge parameters} = V - C = 5 \qquad \text{Physical DOF per isolated cell} = 5\]

Each contact surface is shared between exactly 2 cells, halving the effective count:

\[\frac{\text{DOF/cell}}{\text{holonomies/cell}} = \frac{5}{10} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4} \qquad\Rightarrow\qquad S_\text{BH} = \frac{A}{4\,l_P^2}\]

Both factors are exact from K6 topology: the gauge orbit from the Euler formula \(V - C = 5\), and contact sharing from the foam geometry. The 1 within-G1 cycle gives the subleading logarithmic correction \(S = A/(4l_P^2) + \tfrac{1}{2}\ln(A/l_P^2) + \cdots\), consistent with known quantum gravity results.

8.3 The Taub-NUT Connection

The Hopf fibration S³ → S² is precisely the Taub-NUT geometry, with the NUT parameter \(n\) equal to the fiber length and Berger squashing \(\eta\) scaling as \(n \sim \eta\). The modified entropy formula is:

\[S_\text{K6/QS3}(\eta) = \frac{A}{4\eta\, l_P^2} \times (\text{contact constraint factor})\]

For \(\eta = 1\) and contact factor = 1, \(S = S_\text{BH}\). For \(\eta \to 0\) (dark matter sector), \(S_\text{soft}/S_\text{BH} \to \infty\) — the horizon carries much larger soft hair entropy than Bekenstein-Hawking predicts. Ovalle's gravitational decoupling framework independently supports the multi-sector K6/QS3 structure: the color (K\(_{3,3}\)) and lepton (QS3\(_2\)) sectors decouple in the Ovalle sense, modifying the horizon area law consistently with the K6/QS3 Hodge weights.
