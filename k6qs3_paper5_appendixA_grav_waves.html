A.1 Purpose and Scope

This appendix asks whether K6/QS3's foam spacetime is consistent with gravitational wave emission and, specifically, with the Peters-Mathews quadrupole formula for radiated power. We do not claim a full derivation. We establish that the structure of the formula — its dependence on the time-varying quadrupole moment, its 1/r fall-off, its propagation at c — emerges naturally from foam mechanics. The numerical coefficient (32/5) requires a foam contact integral that we flag as an open computation. An inconsistency at any step would be significant; we find none.

Methodological note: Demonstrating consistency with established results, even without a full derivation, is a meaningful contribution. An inconsistency would falsify K6/QS3. Finding none — with the mechanism sharply identified — is confirmatory evidence and defines precisely what remains to be done.

A.2 Gravitational Waves in GR: The Target

In linearized GR, small perturbations h_μν to flat spacetime satisfy the wave equation. In the transverse-traceless gauge:

□ h̄_μν = -16πG/c⁴ · T_μν

The far-field solution (r >> source size, r >> gravitational wavelength) is dominated by the time-varying mass quadrupole moment Q_ij. The total power radiated is the Peters-Mathews formula:

P = -G/(5c⁵) · ⟨Q̈_ij Q̈^ij⟩ For a circular binary (masses m₁, m₂, separation r, orbital frequency Ω): Q_ij ~ μ r² (orbital geometry factor) where μ = m₁m₂/(m₁+m₂) P = -32/5 · G⁴/c⁵ · m₁²m₂²(m₁+m₂)/r⁵ [Peters-Mathews, 1963]

This was confirmed by Hulse-Taylor (Nobel 1993) to better than 0.1%. Any candidate theory of quantum gravity must reproduce this.

A.3 Foam Mechanics: Setup

In K6/QS3, spacetime is a foam of deformable S³ cells. The round S³ is the vacuum; departures from round = gravitational field. Denote the local Berger squashing parameter of a foam cell at position x by η(x), with η = 1 the undeformed vacuum.

The gravitational field is encoded in the field of deformations:

h(x) ≡ η(x) - 1 |h| << 1 (weak field / linearized regime)

The foam equilibrium condition — minimizing total crease area across cell contacts — is the discrete analog of the Einstein equations (Paper 5, §2). In the linearized regime, this reduces to a condition on h(x) from cell to cell across the contact network.

A.4 Step 1: Linearized Wave Equation from Crease Minimization

Consider a small time-varying deformation h(x,t) of the foam away from a static equilibrium. The crease area functional across a contact surface between cells i and j is:

A_crease(i,j) = A₀ · [1 + κ(h_i - h_j)² + ...]

where A₀ is the undeformed contact area and κ is a geometric stiffness set by the S³ cell radius (the Compton wavelength t_C in Option D calibration). Minimizing total crease area subject to propagation through the contact network gives, in the continuum limit:

∂²h/∂t² - v²∇²h = source

The propagation speed v is the relay chain speed, already established as exactly c (Paper 5, §3; Option D calibration, §3). This is the linearized wave equation for foam perturbations, structurally identical to □h_μν = source in GR.

✓ Consistency check 1: Linearized crease minimization → wave equation with propagation speed exactly c. No free parameters; c emerges from Option D calibration already fixed by electron mass alone.

A.5 Step 2: Far-Field Solution and 1/r Fall-off

The homogeneous wave equation admits outgoing spherical wave solutions. In the foam, "spherical" means averaged over all contact directions of the foam network. For a source localized near the origin, the far-field solution is:

h(r,t) ~ f(t - r/c) / r r >> source size

The 1/r fall-off is a consequence of energy conservation across growing foam shells — the total crease perturbation integrated over a shell at radius r must be constant (no dissipation in vacuum foam), requiring amplitude ∝ 1/r.

✓ Consistency check 2: Far-field foam wave falls as 1/r, propagates at c with retarded time t − r/c. Consistent with GR far-field gravitational wave solution.

A.6 Step 3: Source Term — Rotating Binary as Quadrupole

A static mass M deforms the surrounding foam into a spherically symmetric η(r) profile (discrete Schwarzschild, Paper 5, §4). A rotating binary breaks this spherical symmetry: the combined deformation field has a time-varying angular structure. Decomposing in foam-cell multipoles (the discrete analog of spherical harmonics on the contact network):

h(x,t) = h_monopole(r) + h_dipole(r,t) + h_quadrupole(r,t) + ... h_monopole : static, conserved total mass — no radiation h_dipole : proportional to d/dt(total momentum) = 0 by momentum conservation h_quadrupole: first non-vanishing time-varying term → radiates

This is exactly the multipole structure of GR radiation. Monopole and dipole are forbidden by conservation laws; quadrupole is the leading radiative term.

The foam quadrupole moment is set by the mass distribution's second moment — the same Q_ij as in GR — because the foam deformation field at large r is sourced by the same Newtonian mass distribution in the weak-field limit.

✓ Consistency check 3: Conservation laws (mass, momentum) suppress monopole and dipole radiation in foam mechanics by the same argument as GR. Quadrupole is the leading term. The source Q_ij is identical to the GR expression.

A.7 Step 4: Radiated Power — Structure of the Quadrupole Formula

The power radiated through a distant foam shell is the flux of crease energy outward. In the far field, using h ~ Q̈_ij/r (schematically), the energy flux is:

dP/dΩ ~ (G/c⁵) · |Q̈_ij|² · (angular factor from foam contact geometry)

Integrating over solid angle:

P = (G/c⁵) · C_foam · ⟨Q̈_ij Q̈^ij⟩

where C_foam is a numerical coefficient set by the angular integral over the foam contact network. In GR this integral gives 1/5, yielding the famous factor 32/5 after accounting for the traceless-transverse projection. In K6/QS3, C_foam must be computed from the specific angular structure of the foam contact network — the coordination number and contact angle distribution of S³-cell packing.

Open computation A.1 (Foam contact integral): Compute C_foam from the K6/QS3 foam contact network. The GR result requires C_foam = 1/5 (equivalently, the full coefficient 32/5 in the Peters-Mathews formula). If C_foam ≠ 1/5, K6/QS3 predicts a modified quadrupole formula — testable in principle against Hulse-Taylor and LIGO observations. If C_foam = 1/5, exact consistency is established. This is the central open computation for gravitational wave emission in K6/QS3.

A.8 Step 5: Orbital Decay — Consistency with Hulse-Taylor

The energy carried away by foam waves reduces the binary's orbital energy. By energy conservation:

dE_orbit/dt = -P = -(G/c⁵) · C_foam · ⟨Q̈_ij Q̈^ij⟩

For a circular orbit, E_orbit = −Gm₁m₂/(2r), giving:

ṙ = -C_foam · (64/5) · G³m₁m₂(m₁+m₂) / (c⁵ r³) [Peters formula, modulo C_foam]

The Hulse-Taylor pulsar (PSR B1913+16) provides a precision test: the observed orbital decay matches GR's prediction to better than 0.1% over 40+ years. This sets a tight constraint on C_foam:

|C_foam - 1/5| / (1/5) < 0.001

Computing C_foam and checking this constraint is a direct falsifiability test of K6/QS3's foam geometry.

✓ Consistency check 4: The form of orbital decay — ṙ ∝ G³m₁m₂(m₁+m₂)/r³ — follows from foam mechanics without modification. Only the numerical coefficient C_foam is undetermined. The Hulse-Taylor constraint is sharp and achievable.

A.9 Physical Picture: Why Foam Waves Are Intuitive

In GR, gravitational waves are perturbations of the metric — mathematically clean but physically abstract. In K6/QS3, the picture is more mechanical: a rotating binary continuously reshapes the foam of S³ cells around it. That reshaping propagates outward through cell-to-cell contacts at c. The wave is a traveling pattern of Berger-squashed cells — each cell slightly more or less deformed than vacuum, passing the deformation to its neighbors.

The momentum lost from the orbit is carried away as crease energy — deformation that never returns to the source, permanently altering the far-field foam. This is the discrete analog of Bondi's result that gravitational waves carry energy to infinity, reducing the system's Bondi mass.

Crucially, no aether is implied: the foam cells are not a medium in a background spacetime. They are spacetime. The wave propagates because the cells themselves are the degrees of freedom.
