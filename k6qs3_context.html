1. Scope Statement

K6/QS3 is a discrete quantum field theory program grounded in two geometric objects: the complete graph K6 and the quaternionic 3-sphere QS3. The program derives gauge structure, propagators, Lorentz symmetry, and scattering results from this substrate using a single mass scale fixed by the electron mass. Material properties are an active research front, approached from scratch.

Explicitly out of scope: alpha derivation, gravity and foam geometry, black holes and soft hair, L0/L1 philosophy, Hopf-Galois algebra, cosmology. These are not part of the current program.

2. The Substrate

K6 — Complete Graph

  • 6 vertices, 15 edges
  • Cyclomatic complexity: 10 independent loops
  • Contains K₃,₃ as bipartite subgraph (3+3 vertices, 9 inter-group edges)
  • S₆ automorphism group; Out(S₆) = ℤ₂ (unique among Sₙ, n ≠ 2)

QS3 — Quaternionic 3-Sphere

  • S³ with quaternionic structure; isomorphic to SU(2)
  • Two copies wedged at a basepoint: QS3₁ (quarks, color) and QS3₂ (leptons, colorless)
  • Hopf fibration S³ → S² with fiber S¹ is intrinsic
  • Gamma matrices arise from Weyl spinor structure of QS3 ≅ SU(2)

A single mass scale, the electron mass m_e, fixes all dimensionless ratios. Two derived parameters:

  • λ = m_P / m_e = t_C / l_P = E_P / (m_e c²) = 2.389222 × 10²² — three independent routes, machine precision
  • k = m_e³c⁴ / ℏ² = 5.490 × 10¹¹ J/m²
  • Node spacing: a = t_C = ℏ / (m_e c) = 3.8616 × 10⁻¹³ m (reduced Compton wavelength)
  • Classical electron radius: r_e = α · a = α · t_C = 2.8179 fm ✓
  • Wave speed: v = a√(k/m_e) = c exactly — every factor cancels, verified 12 sig figs
  • UV cutoff: E_max = π m_e c², so E_max / m_e c² = π (exact)
Alpha: α ≈ 1/137.036 is taken from experiment — K6/QS3 makes no claim to derive it. The UV cutoff E_max = π m_e c² and the single scale λ = m_P/m_e are consistent with logarithmic running of α with energy scale. This is an observation of consistency, not a derivation. All scattering results use α as input; they are verifications of geometric consistency, not derivations of α.

Guiding principle: The substrate and gauge structure are derivable from K6/QS3 geometry. The framework is falsifiable.

9. (𝒜, 𝒟) — Operator Duality and Hopf Algebra Identification

𝒜 and 𝒟 form a dual pair acting on Σ. This duality is precisely Hopf algebra duality:

  • 𝒜 provides the coproduct Δ: Σ → Σ ⊗ Σ — each application selects a symmetry-breaking specification, splitting the available set
  • 𝒟 is the antipode S — the formal inverse satisfying m ∘ (S ⊗ id) ∘ Δ = η ∘ ε. 𝒟 composed with the descent returns the native state
  • Antipode is an anti-homomorphism: S(ab) = S(b)S(a) — 𝒟 reverses the order of the descent exactly, recovering history
  • DOF_now + DOF_potential = M is the conservation law of the Hopf algebra counit ε

10. Gauge Symmetry Tower — Open Research Question

Does U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3) admit a genuine tower structure under (𝒜, Σ) — each gauge sector at a distinct level, higher sectors invariant while lower ones descend?

  • U(1), SU(2), SU(3) have distinct geometric origins in K6/QS3 — Hopf fiber S¹, quaternionic generators, K₃,₃. This geometric distinctness is the candidate for level separation.
  • If each gauge sector sits at a genuinely different rank of 𝒜, the tower exists by construction and ρ is non-degenerate across sectors.
  • Specific questions: can U(1) descend while SU(2) × SU(3) remain invariant? Can SU(2) descend while SU(3) remains invariant? Is the ordering unique?
  • K6/QS3 is small enough to answer this exactly — finite computation.
Energy vs momentum: in K6/QS3, spatial translation symmetry is broken by the discrete substrate before any 𝒜 is applied — only crystal momentum survives. Time translation is not broken by the substrate — energy conservation sits at a higher tower level than momentum conservation. Lorentz symmetry sits above both.

11. Material Properties — Active Program

Prior §20 treatment is set aside. The program is rebuilt under the (𝒜, Σ) framework.

Σ = U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3) — full Standard Model gauge symmetry, adopted by assertion. Hodge star duality included by assertion. K6/QS3 already carries all three: U(1) from Hopf fiber, SU(2) from quaternionic generators, SU(3) from K₃,₃.

Relaxation: individual symmetries may be shut down — removed from Σ or partially applied — without disturbing the rest. Shutting down U(1), removing the B sector, or partially applying SU(3) are all valid operations under (𝒜, Σ). Different materials correspond to different subsets of Σ being active.

Local structure: sheaves (algebraic geometry sense) over neighborhoods on QS3. Each K6 node has 5 incident edges — 5 flows. Sheaf assigns local data (e.g. inductance) consistently via restriction maps. Different sections = different physical configurations.

Wedge singularity: genuine singularity where QS3₁ and QS3₂ meet. Seifert surface serves as local band-aid to extend sheaf across it. No deeper role.

Goal: find properties of K6/QS3 invariant under all (𝒜, Σ) — intrinsic substrate facts — and properties that vary, characterizing material classes.
