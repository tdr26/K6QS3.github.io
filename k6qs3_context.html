1. What K6/QS3 Is

A discrete quantum field theory program. The substrate is the complete graph K6 (6 vertices, 15 edges, cyclomatic complexity 10) combined with the quaternionic 3-sphere QS3 (S³ with quaternionic structure, isomorphic to SU(2)). Two copies of QS3 are connected at a wedge basepoint: QS3₁ (quarks, color) and QS3₂ (leptons, colorless). The K₃,₃ bipartite subgraph (3+3 vertices, 9 inter-group edges) carries SU(3) color. The Hopf fibration S³→S² with fiber S¹ is intrinsic to QS3 and carries U(1) electromagnetism.

K6/QS3 is also a model of spacetime: each cell is a deformable 3-sphere, and the foam packing of these cells is curved spacetime. Metric deformation = gravitational field. Equilibrium packing (minimizing crease area) = Einstein equations in discrete form.

Guiding principle: no dimensionless constants inserted by hand. All constants must be derivable from K6/QS3 geometry. The framework is falsifiable.

11. Gravity and Foam Geometry

K6/QS3 cells are deformable 3-spheres packed as a foam. Deformation from round S³ = gravitational field. Equilibrium packing = discrete Einstein equations. Key results:

  • Diffeomorphism invariance: K6 connectivity is topological — all gauge assignments preserved under any smooth deformation.
  • Gravitational falloff: Cells relax toward undeformed S³ away from mass source. Discrete Schwarzschild 1/r profile.
  • Orbits = stationary phase: Relay clumps follow stationary-phase paths through non-uniform foam = geodesics = orbits.
  • Contact surfaces = Israel junctions: Holonomy continuity across cell boundaries. Matter shells = high-crease-density regions.
  • Vacuum squashing: Berger sphere parameter η deforms the round S³. Shifts Hodge weights. Candidate mechanism for alpha residual.

12. Black Hole Information: Soft Hair

Interior-exterior entanglement mechanism: K6 is 5-edge-connected — 5 simultaneous edge failures required to sever any vertex. Local thermal fluctuations cannot do this. Interior and exterior remain topologically connected through foam contact topology. Information stored in topological integers (holonomy winding numbers, Wilson loops) — not in metric degrees of freedom. Topology preserved through horizon; metric is not.

Soft hair = K6/QS3 holonomies at horizon: Each Planck-scale cell on the horizon carries 10 independent loop holonomies: 1 U(1) + 3 SU(2) + 6 SU(3). These record gauge charges of every infalling particle. Topological integers — cannot be erased by smooth interior deformations.

\[ S_\text{soft} = 10 \times \frac{A}{l_P^2} = 40 \times S_\text{BH} \qquad \frac{S_\text{soft}}{S_\text{BH}} = \frac{10}{1/4} = 40 \]

Both scale as A/l_P² (same universality class). Factor 40 = 4 × 10 = (BH area law denominator) × (K6 cyclomatic complexity).

Key prediction: Foam contact constraints (holonomy continuity across cell boundaries) must reduce the count by exactly 40 to recover S_BH = A/(4l_P²). If this follows from K6/QS3 contact topology, the famous factor 1/4 in the area law is the foam contact constraint factor. This is the central open computation.

K6/QS3 vs HPS soft hair: HPS covers gravitons only, continuous BMS symmetry, origin of 1/4 unclear. K6/QS3 covers full Standard Model gauge content, discrete holonomies (topologically stable), 1/4 = foam contact constraint. K6/QS3 is a discrete microscopic realization of HPS.

13. Black Hole Evaporation: Phase Structure

Hawking temperature at the BH threshold (M = m_P²/m_e): k_BT_H = m_ec²/π = 162 keV. Edge energies ★×m_ec² range from 305 to 681 keV. At the threshold, E_edge/k_BT_H ≈ 15–33: all edges frozen active. Statistical fluctuations (snap-reformation dynamics) occur at a different, lower mass:

\[ M_\text{critical} = \frac{M_\text{threshold}}{8\pi\,\star_\text{within}} = \frac{m_P^2/m_e}{12\arccos(1/\sqrt{10})} \approx 3.5\times10^{13}\;\text{kg} \]

Three regimes: M > 3.5×10¹³ kg → K6 frozen (all SM structure intact at horizon, includes all astrophysical BHs). M ≈ 3.5×10¹³ kg → within-group edges fluctuating (Fermi statistics dynamical). M < 1.6×10¹³ kg → all edges fluctuating (SM structure dissolving). Evaporation passes through these regimes in order as the BH loses mass.
