1. Scope Statement

K6/QS3 is a discrete quantum field theory program grounded in two geometric objects: the complete graph K6 and the quaternionic 3-sphere QS3. The program derives gauge structure, propagators, Lorentz symmetry, and scattering results from this substrate using a single mass scale fixed by the electron mass. Material properties are an active research front, approached from scratch.

Explicitly out of scope: alpha derivation, gravity and foam geometry, black holes and soft hair, L0/L1 philosophy, Hopf-Galois algebra, cosmology. These are not part of the current program.

9. (𝒜, 𝒟) — Operator Duality, Hopf Algebra, and Galois Tower

𝒜 and 𝒟 form a dual pair acting on Σ. Precise identification:

  • 𝒜 = multiplication: H ⊗ H → H. Each application collapses a tensor product of symmetry states, descending the tower one level, releasing DOF. 𝒜ⁿ(Y) → X + released DOF.
  • 𝒟 = comultiplication: H → H ⊗ H. Each application expands a state back into a tensor product, restoring DOF_potential. 𝒟ᵐ(X) → Y + added DOF.
  • Antipode emerges from composition of 𝒜 and 𝒟, not as a primitive. Satisfies m ∘ (S ⊗ id) ∘ Δ = η ∘ ε.
  • Counit ε: DOF_now + DOF_potential = M is the conservation law.

Shortest Path Argument — n = m

Restrict 𝒜ⁿ to the shortest collapse path (minimum n) and 𝒟ᵐ to the shortest recovery path (minimum m). K6 is a finite undirected graph — shortest paths exist (guaranteed by finiteness) and distances are symmetric. Therefore n = m by symmetry of the undirected metric. Strict Hopf antipode is restored without quasi-Hopf machinery.

Coset Factoring — Path Ambiguity

Multiple shortest paths of length n may exist between two tower levels. Collect them into a coset of the path group under the equivalence "same length, same endpoints." Factor by this coset. The quotient identifies all shortest paths as a single representative — path ambiguity disappears at the level of the quotient, exactly as in Galois theory where orbits replace individual automorphisms.

Galois Tower Structure

Each linguistic or gauge layer is a fixed-point subspace under a subgroup of 𝒜-actions. Higher layers (semantics, Lorentz symmetry) are invariant under lower-level transformations. The tower is non-degenerate when symmetries at each level are independent — dependent symmetries collapse to the same level.

Knowing Σ_current and Σ_target is sufficient to compute the shortest recovery path — the path is derivable from the states. Path history is redundant. 10. Gauge Symmetry Tower — Open Research Question

Does U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3) admit a genuine tower structure under (𝒜, Σ) — each gauge sector at a distinct level, higher sectors invariant while lower ones descend?

  • U(1), SU(2), SU(3) have distinct geometric origins in K6/QS3 — Hopf fiber S¹, quaternionic generators, K₃,₃. This geometric distinctness is the candidate for level separation.
  • If each gauge sector sits at a genuinely different rank of 𝒜, the tower exists by construction and ρ is non-degenerate across sectors.
  • Specific questions: can U(1) descend while SU(2) × SU(3) remain invariant? Can SU(2) descend while SU(3) remains invariant? Is the ordering unique?
  • K6/QS3 is small enough to answer this exactly — finite computation.
Energy vs momentum: in K6/QS3, spatial translation symmetry is broken by the discrete substrate before any 𝒜 is applied — only crystal momentum survives. Time translation is not broken by the substrate — energy conservation sits at a higher tower level than momentum conservation. Lorentz symmetry sits above both.
