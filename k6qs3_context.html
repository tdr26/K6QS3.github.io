1. What K6/QS3 Is

A discrete quantum field theory program. The substrate is the complete graph K6 (6 vertices, 15 edges, cyclomatic complexity 10) combined with the quaternionic 3-sphere QS3 (S³ with quaternionic structure, isomorphic to SU(2)). Two copies of QS3 are connected at a wedge basepoint: QS3₁ (quarks, color) and QS3₂ (leptons, colorless). The K₃,₃ bipartite subgraph (3+3 vertices, 9 inter-group edges) carries SU(3) color. The Hopf fibration S³→S² with fiber S¹ is intrinsic to QS3 and carries U(1) electromagnetism.

K6/QS3 is also a model of spacetime: each cell is a deformable 3-sphere, and the foam packing of these cells is curved spacetime. Metric deformation = gravitational field. Equilibrium packing (minimizing crease area) = Einstein equations in discrete form.

Guiding principle: no dimensionless constants inserted by hand. All constants must be derivable from K6/QS3 geometry. The framework is falsifiable.

Purpose of predictions and cosmology sections: to provide independent motivation for the validity of the model — each confirmed prediction strengthens the geometric origin of the Standard Model constants. The cosmology predictions (CMB, proton mass, BH information) are long-range; the condensed matter predictions (Weyl semimetals, QHE) are near-term and directly testable.

9. Bell's Inequality: Topological Necessity

11. Berger Squashing: The η Parameter

The Berger squashing parameter η is central to three connected phenomena:

  1. Alpha residual: Δ(1/α) ≈ −η/(3π)[6 ln(1+η) + 9 ln(1−η/2)]. The remaining gap of −0.854 requires η ≈ 0.84.
  2. Dark matter candidate: ★_{U(1)}(η) = 1/(4η). As η→0 the Hopf fiber degenerates, c₁→0, EM coupling vanishes but mass (from SU(2) base) is preserved. A massive, electromagnetically neutral WIMP emerges from the geometry.
  3. Weyl semimetal impedance tensor (Paper 4, §8 — NEW):
    • Conductivity: σ̂(η,θ_B) = σ₀[I + (γ/η)cos²θ_B n̂⊗n̂]
    • Longitudinal impedance: Z_∥(η,θ_B) = R₀ / (1 + γcos²θ_B/η)
    • Transverse impedance: Z_⊥ = R₀ (no correction)
    • Hall impedance: Z_H(η) = R_K·η
    If a Weyl semimetal Hall experiment measures Z_H/R_K = 0.84 ± 0.01 at zero strain, this independently fixes η = 0.84 and closes the alpha calculation. Two different observables, one geometric parameter.

The three η-dependent phenomena are not independent — they are all faces of the same Berger deformation of the K6/QS3 cell metric.
